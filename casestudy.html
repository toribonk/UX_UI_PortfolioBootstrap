Case Study - Recipe App







OVERVIEW:

Life can be busy and chores like grocery shopping and cooking can be overwhelming. We wanted to step aside from the traditional recipe app where users just save their favorite meals. We had a goal to create a bit more universal food app for users who prefer home cooked meals. We want to understand how people start planning, cooking, deciding their meals and what issues they are facing so we could build the product that resolves their frustration with the features they need for their meals.

ROLE:

UX Reseacher & Designer

TEAM:

Team Project

TOOLS:

Miro, Figma, Adobe XD















USER RESEARCH:

We will use a mix of 1on1 interviews and a survey to gather qualitative data on users current recipe usage. The collected data will be compared and analyzed to understand how recipe apps and websites are coming up short for the users.



DEFINITION & IDEATION:

During our research, we observed that many recipe apps did not have the flexibility and personalization needed by our users which causes them frustration when cooking. Users need an easier way to find dinner ideas so that they can enjoy their time at home without stress and time-consuming recipes. This is why we are determined to think of a way to simplify this process and reduce the time from thinking of a meal to eating the meal. The endless wondering of what to eat is a burden to all of us and we believe that this app will help streamline the process and save time.

2) Add New Recipe to Recipe Book.

REFLECTION:

If we had more time, we would have like to make the app more personalized for the user by adding the pantry tracking feature. We realized as we started to make our app that we were trying to do too much. Our biggest challenge was figuring out how to collaborate 100% digitally. We solved this by having additional Zoom meet-ups / work sessions outside of class hours and using Figma to be able to work at the same time.
